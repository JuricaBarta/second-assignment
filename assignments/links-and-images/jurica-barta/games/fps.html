Call of Duty & Fortnite

"Call of Duty: Warzone" and "Fortnite" are both popular battle royale games.
"Warzone" is a free-to-play game developed by Infinity Ward and Raven Software, released in 2020.
The game is set in the fictional city of Verdansk and allows up to 150 players to compete in a last-man-standing battle.
The game features realistic gunplay, vehicle combat, and a unique Gulag system, where players can fight their way back into the game if they are eliminated.

"Fortnite" is a free-to-play game developed by Epic Games, released in 2017.
The game features a cartoonish art style and allows up to 100 players to compete in a last-man-standing battle.
The game features unique building mechanics, where players can construct structures and fortifications on the fly,
adding an extra layer of strategy to the gameplay. The game also features a variety of modes,
including creative modes where players can build and share their own custom maps.

Both "Warzone" and "Fortnite" have large player bases and offer unique gameplay experiences.
"Warzone" is known for its realistic gunplay and tense atmosphere,
while "Fortnite" is known for its colorful art style, building mechanics, and unique events.
Both games offer regular updates and new content, keeping players engaged and coming back for more.
