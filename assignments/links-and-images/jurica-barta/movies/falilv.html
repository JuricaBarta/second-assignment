Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas

"Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas" is a dark comedy film directed by Terry Gilliam, released in 1998.
The movie is based on the classic novel by Hunter S. Thompson and follows the drug-fueled journey of journalist Raoul Duke and his lawyer
Dr. Gonzo as they travel to Las Vegas to cover a motorcycle race and indulge in a variety of mind-altering substances.

The film stars Johnny Depp as Duke and Benicio del Toro as Gonzo, who deliver over-the-top and hilarious performances
that capture the outrageous and surreal nature of the story. The movie is known for its psychedelic visuals, trippy sound design,
and irreverent humor that skewers the excesses and contradictions of American culture.

"Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas" is a cult classic that has gained a devoted following over the years,
and is widely regarded as a masterpiece of gonzo filmmaking. The movie is a wild and anarchic ride that celebrates the spirit of
counterculture and rebellion, and remains as relevant today as it was when it was first released.
